About Me

I am currently an M.S. student at Purdue focused on transforming easily capturable media into metrically and physically accurate, editable, and interactible 3D mesh-based simulations. My goal is to digitize indoor environments in ways that improve the data robots use to understand and reason about the world.

Research Interests

  • Generative modeling: building controllable models that can synthesize plausible scene geometry, appearance, and dynamics from limited observations.
  • Perception: improving how robotic systems infer structure, object properties, and uncertainty from real-world visual input.
  • Visual reasoning: connecting perception to higher-level scene understanding for grounded decision-making and interaction in 3D environments.

Background

I previously earned my B.S. in Computer Engineering at Purdue, where I worked in the VAA and VIPER labs. I am currently the Co-Founder of UpLiv Technologies.

Beyond Work

Outside of work, I enjoy hockey, golf, and soccer, competing in powerlifting competitions, and playing the piano.
